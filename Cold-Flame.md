# COLD FLAME

DRAMA TV presents a **3 a.m. drive-thru tragedy**, staged in your terminal.
BURGER MAJESTY, off the interstate. One lonely night-shift cook. An empty dining
room of two-tops humming under dead fluorescents. A single burger going cold
beneath a heat lamp that burned out weeks ago. He confesses everything to the
drive-thru speaker; only static answers back. The shake machine is, of course,
down. Then **THE REVEAL** — the flame-broiler sputters once in the dark, a truth
about *low-temperature* and *lonely* people lands, and the room is **FLOORED**.

The order comes up… for **you**. But no one is coming to the counter.

The beats:

1. **3:00 A.M. — BURGER MAJESTY, OFF THE INTERSTATE**
2. **THE LONELY SHIFT** — one worker, an empty room, the freezer hum
3. **THE COLD BURGER** — a confession to a burger under a dead heat lamp
4. **THE DRIVE-THRU CONFESSIONAL** — he speaks; the speaker answers in static
5. **THE SHAKE MACHINE IS DOWN** — it is always down
6. **THE REVEAL** — *…you were the low-temperature one all along* — FLOORED
7. **ORDER UP FOR {YOU}** — …but no one comes to the counter
8. **NOW SERVING: NO ONE** — fade to a cold, greasy static

```powershell
.\Cold-Flame.ps1                       # the night shift, forever -- press any key to clock out
.\Cold-Flame.ps1 -Shifts 2             # two tragedies, then the lobby closes
.\Cold-Flame.ps1 -Calm                 # a warmer ending: the flame catches, a customer arrives
.\Cold-Flame.ps1 -Fast                 # snappier holds (for the impatient)
.\Cold-Flame.ps1 -Silent               # no beeps (a quiet, empty lobby)
.\Cold-Flame.ps1 -Storyboard -Scenes 1 -Seed 42   # the static montage above
```
