# MAKE A SCENE

DRAMA TV's melodrama of public outbursts, staged in your terminal. It begins
ordinary — a bistro, a self-checkout, the 8:15 to Droitwich. Then something
NEBALOSE happens (the soup is cold; the receipt is, somehow, WARM) and a
perfectly normal person decides, profusely, to **MAKE A SCENE.** The chair
SCRAPES. The room turns. **RU JEALOSE THAT I'M MAKING A SCENE?** The
SCENE-O-METER pins, the phones come out, four stars are awarded, and the room is
FLOORED. Then they storm out — the door does not slam, it auto-closes — and lo:
**THER WIL BE CONSEQUINCES.** The SCENE, however, REMAINS.

> **On spelling:** the misspellings are deliberate. NEBALOSE, JEALOSE, NEVORE,
> CONSEQUINCES, AGAN — that is the show's voice. On purpose.

The beats:

1. **THE CALM BEFORE** — a mundane, candlelit, nebalose setting
2. **THE TRIGGER** — something nebalose (the soup is cold; the oat milk was, in fact, NORMAL milk)
3. **THE STANDING UP** — the chair SCRAPES; the room half-turns
4. **THE SCENE** — the outburst escalates; the SCENE-O-METER pins
5. **EVERYONE IS WATCHING** — a man at table four films it; FOUR STARS and a review; a child asks why
6. **FLOORED** — the room is levelled
7. **THE EXIT** — they storm out (the door does not slam — auto-close); the SCENE REMAINS
8. **THER WIL BE CONSEQUINCES** — the stamp comes down (you are a person of interest) → static

```powershell
.\Make-A-Scene.ps1                      # scenes, forever -- press any key (that's a scene)
.\Make-A-Scene.ps1 -Scenes 2            # two scenes, then off-air
.\Make-A-Scene.ps1 -Calm                # they breathe, and DON'T make a scene -- a quiet triumph
.\Make-A-Scene.ps1 -Fast                # snappier holds (for the impatient)
.\Make-A-Scene.ps1 -Silent              # no beeps (a scene, in mime)
.\Make-A-Scene.ps1 -Storyboard -Acts 1 -Seed 42   # the static montage above
```
